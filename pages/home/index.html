AI-Powered Energy Literacy for Urban Residents

This project proposes a lightweight AI-driven tool that helps everyday occupants understand and improve the energy performance of their homes. It draws from building science fundamentals such as heat transfer, solar gain, and ventilation to offer actionable feedback for renters and homeowners.

Why This Matters

Political Impact

Makes energy knowledge public, not just expert-controlled. Democratizes access to building science and energy literacy, empowering communities to make informed decisions about their built environment.

Social Impact

Empowers underserved communities with tools for energy literacy and cost savings. Addresses energy justice concerns by making efficiency tools accessible to all, regardless of technical background.

Cultural Impact

Turns energy use into something visible, knowable, and actionable for everyday people. Shifts the narrative around energy consumption from invisible infrastructure to tangible, manageable systems.

Core Research Questions

1. AI-Driven Micro-Scale Design

How can AI models trained on architectural and environmental data provide meaningful suggestions for energy-saving behavior at the room or unit level? This question explores the intersection of machine learning and building science.

2. Accessible Design Tools

Can we design a tool that gives micro-scale design advice to non-architects in plain language? This research investigates the democratization of architectural knowledge through user-friendly interfaces.

3. Citizen Data Integration

How might this tool integrate with citizen-collected data from sensors or simple user-reported habits? This explores the potential for community-driven energy monitoring and optimization.

Project Overview

Research Context

This project emerges from the intersection of computational design, environmental psychology, and data science. It builds on existing energy efficiency research while introducing novel approaches to user engagement and knowledge democratization.

Target Users

Primary users include urban renters, homeowners, building managers, and energy educators. The tool is designed to serve diverse communities with varying levels of technical expertise and energy literacy.

Expected Outcomes

Immediate impact includes improved energy literacy and cost savings for users. Long-term benefits include community energy initiatives, policy advocacy, and sustainable behavior pattern development.
