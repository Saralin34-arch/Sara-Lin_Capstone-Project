RESEARCH QUESTION

How can we make building science accessible to everyday New Yorkers, and turn passive discomfort into informed, local action?

PROJECT PROPOSAL

Building on the ideas explored in my previous Smart Citizen project—which enabled individuals to collect and share environmental data through open-source platforms—this project proposes an AI-powered building science tool designed for everyday users.

The tool will exist as a mobile or web-based application that allows individuals to analyze the energy performance and comfort conditions of their own spaces. Rather than requiring specialized hardware or technical expertise, the platform will guide users through a process of observation, input, and AI-supported analysis.

Users will have the option to upload and share their results to a collective platform, creating a crowdsourced dataset of occupant experiences and building behaviors. Over time, this shared knowledge base can help inform community-led improvements—and potentially contribute to broader energy policy advocacy.

Through this system, the project aims to bridge the gap between professional building science and everyday lived experience—offering practical tools for personal insight, while contributing to larger-scale environmental impact.

By designing for accessibility, participation, and data-sharing, the platform seeks to empower individuals and communities to become active agents of change in the energy transition.

RHETORICAL ARGUMENT

This project challenges the idea that knowledge about energy and thermal comfort should be limited to experts. By using design to translate complex building and environmental data into simple, visual feedback, it makes energy impact understandable and accessible to everyone.

THE CHALLENGE

Technical Skills Development: The project requires advanced computational design skills, including data visualization, interactive web development, and AI integration. While I have foundational knowledge in computational design and mapping systems, developing a sophisticated AI-powered platform presents a significant learning curve in machine learning implementation and user interface design.

Data Collection and Validation: Creating a comprehensive dataset of building energy patterns and user comfort preferences is a major challenge. This involves not only gathering existing data from various sources (DOE, ASHRAE, NYC building records) but also validating and standardizing this information to ensure accuracy for AI training and user recommendations.

User Experience Design: Translating complex building science concepts into accessible, visual feedback requires advanced UX/UI skills. The challenge lies in creating an interface that is both scientifically accurate and user-friendly for non-experts, balancing technical depth with accessibility.

Interdisciplinary Collaboration: The project spans multiple domains—building science, computational design, AI development, and user experience. Finding collaborators with expertise in these diverse areas and coordinating their contributions presents logistical and communication challenges.

Current Skills Assessment: My strengths lie in computational mapping, data analysis, and architectural design thinking. However, I need to develop skills in AI/ML implementation, advanced web development, and user testing methodologies. My plan involves focused learning in these areas through online courses, workshops, and collaboration with experts in AI and UX design.
