HISTORICAL LINEAGE

The Energy Literacy Gap: A Democratic Problem

My work emerges from a critical gap in the democratization of building science knowledge. While the field has evolved from expert-only tools in the 1970s to sophisticated computational platforms today, there remains a fundamental disconnect between professional expertise and everyday building occupants. This trajectory continues the citizen science movement but specifically addresses the democratic issue of knowledge access in building science.

Tools and Theory: From Expert-Only to Accessible

The historical trajectory I'm continuing began with EnergyPlus (1999) and professional BIM tools, which were designed for trained engineers and architects. Recent developments like the Smart Citizen Platform and Public Lab's DIY environmental monitoring represent a shift toward democratization, but these tools still require technical expertise. My project advances this trajectory by creating an AI interface that translates complex building science into accessible guidance.

Political and Societal Context

The energy crisis of the 1970s and climate urgency today create a political imperative for energy literacy. However, current approaches rely heavily on professional expertise, creating a knowledge asymmetry where those most affected by energy costs have the least access to actionable information. My work addresses this democratic deficit by empowering everyday citizens to understand and act on their building's energy behavior.

Design and Aesthetics: Visual Translation

I'm continuing the trajectory of visual representation in building science, from early energy modeling outputs to modern data visualization. However, I'm advancing this by focusing on user experience design that makes complex data understandable to non-experts. The aesthetic approach prioritizes clarity and accessibility over technical sophistication.

COMMUNITY OF PRACTICE

Precedent Study: Smart Citizen Platform

What they're doing: Open-source environmental monitoring with community data collection and visualization.

My relationship: While I admire their open-source approach and community focus, I'm creating an alternative proposal that goes beyond data collection to provide actionable guidance. The Smart Citizen Platform shows data but doesn't translate it into practical strategies for energy reduction and comfort improvement.

Academic Research: Harvard Healthy Buildings Program

What they're asking: How do indoor environmental conditions affect occupant health and performance?

My position: I'm building on their research findings but reinterpreting their approach by focusing on practical application rather than academic study. Their work informs my understanding of indoor comfort factors, but I'm translating their research into accessible tools for everyday users.

Professional Tools: ASHRAE and Energy Modeling Software

What they're doing: Developing sophisticated energy modeling tools and standards for building professionals.

My critique: These tools are excellent for experts but create a barrier for everyday building occupants. I'm contesting the assumption that building science knowledge should remain in the domain of professionals. My project creates a bridge between professional expertise and public need.

Citizen Science: Public Lab and DIY Environmental Monitoring

What they're doing: Empowering communities with DIY tools for environmental monitoring and data collection.

My advancement: I'm advancing their approach by adding AI-powered analysis and personalized recommendations. While they focus on data collection, I'm focusing on translating that data into actionable insights for energy reduction and comfort improvement.

Commercial Platforms: Energy Monitoring Apps

What they're doing: Providing basic energy usage tracking and simple tips.

My subversion: I'm subverting their approach by providing deeper building science knowledge rather than just usage tracking. Instead of simple tips, I'm offering professional-level analysis made accessible through AI translation.

Audience Relationship

Community of Practice: Building scientists, energy modelers, sustainability professionals, citizen scientists

My Project Audience: NYC homeowners and renters, small-scale designers, community groups

Relationship: My audience is different from my community of practice. I'm creating a bridge between the sophisticated knowledge of building science professionals and the practical needs of everyday building occupants. This positions my work as a translation service rather than a contribution to the existing professional discourse.
