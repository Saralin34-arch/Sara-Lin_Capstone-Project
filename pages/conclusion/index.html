PROJECT OVERVIEW + MOTIVATION

Indoor comfort isn't a luxury, it's a basic condition of daily life.

But comfort is shaped by complex and invisible systems: heat transfer, insulation, airflow, solar exposure. When residents can't access or interpret this data, they're left guessing.

In NYC, where over 70% of carbon emissions come from buildings, the urgency is growing.

Energy prices are rising, and climate extremes are putting more strain on both infrastructure and residents. Yet most modeling tools are built for professionalsâ€”not for those actually living in these buildings.

This project is motivated by the need to change that.

CONCLUSION

This project represents a step toward democratizing building science knowledge and empowering everyday New Yorkers to take control of their indoor environments.

Through the development of an AI-powered platform that translates complex building science into accessible insights, we can bridge the gap between professional expertise and public understanding.

The combination of computational design, citizen science, and AI technology creates a unique opportunity to address both individual comfort needs and broader environmental challenges.

As we face increasing energy costs and climate extremes, tools like this become essential for building resilient, comfortable, and sustainable communities.
