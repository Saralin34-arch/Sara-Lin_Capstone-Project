Research Context
This project addresses the growing need for accessible, AI-driven feedback tools that help everyday people improve their indoor comfort and reduce bills without requiring major renovation. It aligns with Data Feminism principles by shifting design agency toward residents and making energy knowledge transparent and contextual.
Core Research Questions
  • How can AI models trained on architectural and environmental data provide meaningful suggestions for energy-saving behavior at the room or unit level?
  • Can we design a tool that gives micro-scale design advice to non-architects in plain language?
  • How might this tool integrate with citizen-collected data from sensors or simple user-reported habits?
Technology Stack
Frontend: p5.js for interactive floor plan drawing, D3.js for data visualizations, Chart.js for time-series graphs
AI Engine: Pretrained models (XGBoost or lightweight neural nets) for predicting room-level heat gain/loss
Data Sources: Local weather APIs, utility cost models, architectural data
